Cubby · privacy

What's in your bins is your business.

Cubby is an inventory app for iPhone and iPad that helps you find things stored in bins. This page describes what data Cubby handles and what it does with it. The short version: your inventory, photos, and tags stay on your device unless you explicitly share them.

Last updated: 28 June 2026

What Cubby collects

Nothing. Cubby has no accounts, no sign-in, no analytics, no crash-reporting SDK, no advertising identifiers, and no third-party SDKs of any kind. The developer receives no data from the app — there is no server for it to be sent to.

Where your inventory lives

Your racks, bins, items, quantities, and activity history are stored in a database on your device. Item photos are saved as files in the app's own storage area. None of this is uploaded or transmitted by Cubby. If you choose to share something — for example a printed label PDF via the iOS share sheet, to Files, AirDrop, Mail, or a printer — that destination's privacy policy governs the file from that point on.

Camera access

Cubby requests camera access so you can photograph an item and let the app suggest a name for it. Identification uses Apple's on-device Vision framework: the analysis runs entirely on your device, camera frames are used only while you are adding an item, and no image is uploaded anywhere. The photo you keep with an item is stored locally with that item and nowhere else. Decline camera access and you can still add and name items by hand.

NFC and QR tags

Cubby reads and writes NFC tags and reads QR codes on your racks and bins. A tag holds only a link that identifies a bin or rack — for example https://gotcubby.com/b/<id>. The tag never contains the bin's contents; the contents are always looked up locally on your device from that identifier. Anyone who taps one of your tags with another phone sees only the link, not what is in the bin. Reading a tag requires no network connection.

Barcodes you capture

If you scan a product barcode while adding an item, Cubby stores the barcode value locally with that item. It does not look the code up in any online database — there is no product lookup and no network request. The barcode is never used as the item's name.

Optional iCloud sync

Cubby can optionally sync your inventory across your own devices using your personal iCloud account. This is off by default; it does nothing until you turn it on in Settings. When enabled, your data moves through your iCloud — Apple's encrypted service, governed by Apple's privacy policy — and not through any server belonging to Cubby or its developer. Turn it off and your data stays on the device. If you have iCloud Backup enabled at the system level, your Cubby data may also be included in your encrypted iCloud Backup, which is likewise governed by Apple.

On-device processing

Everything Cubby does — storing your inventory, identifying items, generating QR labels, building printable label PDFs, and searching — runs on your device. There is no server-side component and no telemetry.

Children

Cubby is rated 4+. It does not collect any information from anyone, so it does not collect information from children either.

Changes

If a future version of Cubby changes what data it handles, this policy will be updated and the "Last updated" date revised before that version ships.
